# Telecom-Customer-Churn-Analysis-

Insights & Findings

Key Observations from the Dashboard
The analysis shows that customer churn in this dataset is "relatively uniform across most segments" with no single dominant driver.

---

1. Overall Churn Level

The overall churn rate is "26.7%", indicating a moderate level of customer attrition.
Insight: 
Roughly 1 in 4 customers churn, but this behavior is broadly consistent across groups.

---

2. Limited Impact of Contract Type

Churn rates across contract types (Month-to-month, One year, Two year) are **very similar (~26–27%)**
Insight: 
Contract type does **not significantly influence churn** in this dataset, contrary to common expectations.

---

3. No Strong Relationship with Monthly Charges

* Customers with higher total monthly charges do **not show higher churn**
* In fact, non-churned customers contribute more to total charges
Insight:
Pricing does **not appear to be a key driver of churn** in this case.

---

4. Demographic Factors Have Minimal Influence

* Gender shows **negligible difference** in churn rates
* Senior and non-senior customers have **almost identical churn proportions**
Insight:
Customer demographics (age and gender) have **limited impact on churn behavior**

---

5. Payment Method Shows No Meaningful Effect

* Churn rates across payment methods are nearly identical (~26–27%)
Insight:
Payment method does **not significantly affect churn likelihood**

---

6. Tenure Distribution Insight

* Higher churn counts are observed in longer tenure groups (24+ months)
* However, this likely reflects **larger group sizes rather than higher churn risk**
Insight:
Tenure alone does not clearly indicate higher churn risk without considering proportions.

---

Overall Conclusion

The analysis suggests that churn behavior in this dataset is "not strongly driven by any single factor".

* Most variables (contract type, demographics, payment method, pricing) show "minimal variation in churn rates"
* Churn appears to be "evenly distributed across customer segments"

Key Takeaway:
Customer churn in this dataset may be influenced by factors "not captured in the available features" such as customer satisfaction, service quality, or external competition.
